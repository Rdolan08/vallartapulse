Title

Refine Stay vs Rent v1 copy and add optional range display for high-value stays

⸻

Summary

Tighten the Stay vs Rent v1 card copy to strengthen clarity and impact, and optionally introduce a range display (instead of a point estimate) for high-value date ranges (e.g., NYE, Christmas).

This is a pure frontend refinement. No pricing logic, backend changes, or tracking additions.

⸻

Current Behavior

The card shows:

“Based on comparable rentals in [hood] for the same dates.
This is an estimate of what the stay would earn if rented — not a price recommendation.”

Headline shows a single point estimate derived from:

* summary.stay_window_total
* or fallback stay_window_median × nights

⸻

Problem

1. Copy is slightly defensive (“not a price recommendation”) and reduces impact.
2. Point estimate can feel overly precise, especially for high-value stays.
3. Opportunity-cost framing could be clearer and more direct.

⸻

Proposed Changes

1. Copy refinement (required)

Replace current body copy with:

Based on comparable rentals in [hood] for the same dates.
Estimated rental value: what this stay could earn if rented.

Notes:

* Remove “not a price recommendation”
* Keep tone factual and neutral
* Maintain opportunity-cost framing

⸻

2. Range display for high-value stays (optional, behind simple condition)

For high-value date ranges (e.g., total ≥ configurable threshold, e.g. $1,500):

Display:

Estimated rental value: $3,200–$4,100

Instead of:

Estimated rental value: $3,450

Range construction (v1 simple approach)

* center = current total
* band = ±10–15% (or small fixed band, e.g. ±$200–$500 depending on magnitude)
* round to clean numbers (no cents)

Notes:

* Do NOT introduce new backend fields
* Compute entirely in frontend
* Keep logic simple and conservative

⸻

Non-Goals

* No pricing-engine changes
* No new endpoints
* No tracking additions
* No event logic integration
* No actions/buttons added to the card

⸻

Acceptance Criteria

* Updated copy renders correctly for all locales via t()
* Card still suppresses when:
    * no usable stay-window data
    * stay_window_samples < 5
* Range display only appears when threshold condition met
* No visual overlap or confusion with Forward-demand card
* No regressions in pricing tool behavior

⸻

Priority

Low (UX polish), but high leverage for perception and clarity—especially on high-value dates.

⸻

Context

This card is designed to:

* frame opportunity cost (stay vs rent)
* remain informational (no actions)
* complement Forward-demand without overlapping responsibilities
